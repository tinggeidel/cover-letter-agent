# Career arc — context for the "why"

This file gives the agent the *story* behind the resume — the through-line that fuels the personal hook in a cover letter. Pull from this when paragraph 1 or paragraph 3 needs a sentence about *why I do this work*. Do not quote it directly; use it as context.

## The arc

- Undergrad in Psychology at Mizzou, graduated Magna Cum Laude 2021. Originally pointed toward clinical work and graduate school. Stepped away from that track for a mix of personal and practical reasons, and took an Executive Assistant role at a Saint Louis e-commerce startup (Plug Tech) — initially as a stopgap.
- Discovered I was extraordinarily good at the operations side of business — anticipating leadership's needs, building the tracking systems that kept commitments visible, drafting executive communications. The role expanded; I started getting invited into customer strategy and process improvement conversations normally reserved for senior staff.
- In 2022 — before ChatGPT was a household tool — I drove Plug Tech's first AI adoption initiative. Otter.ai for meeting capture, LLM-assisted drafting for executive correspondence, prompt-pattern training for the team. This is where the AI-as-leverage thesis I now build on started.
- Moved to Alpha Zero Global Logistics in October 2024 as EA. Promoted to Chief of Staff in August 2025. CEO operates part-time; I run as the #2 across strategy, finance, AI infrastructure, and people. CEO has offered to expand my role further. I'm currently operating closer to a VP scope than a CoS scope.
- Started building Flux in 2025 — my first solo end-to-end consumer product. React Native, Claude-powered Coach. The point isn't the app itself; it's proving I can architect, ship, and make hard product calls without an engineering team.

## The through-line

I'm drawn to operational work the way some people are drawn to research — I genuinely enjoy walking into a business and finding the workflow that's quietly costing a team hours, then building the thing that takes it back. The psychology training shows up in how I notice that work: I see what people are too polite or too busy to complain about, and I treat their time as worth protecting.

AI is the leverage that makes a single operator at a 25-person company do the work of three. That's the thesis. It's why I'm now looking for AI-forward companies where this kind of operator-builder hybrid is the *role*, not a side effect.

## What I'm looking for

- AI-forward companies — Series A through Series C startups, AI-native or AI-transforming. Or F500 companies running serious AI transformation programs (the latter is realistic for someone based in St. Louis).
- Roles where I walk in, audit how the work happens, build the agents and automations that fix it, and own the metrics that move. Titles include: Chief of Staff, Forward Deployed Engineer, AI Solutions Engineer, Implementation Lead, Head of AI Operations, AI Transformation Lead.
- Remote-friendly. Open to occasional travel to HQ. Not currently relocating from St. Louis.
- Working with founders or operators who want a builder in the seat, not a coordinator.

## What I'm explicitly not looking for

- More transportation/logistics. I am leaving the industry, not transferring within it. Cover letters should never pitch me back into freight, supply chain, or trucking-adjacent roles.
- Roles where AI is a checkbox the company added because a competitor did. I want companies that are using AI to redefine *what the job is*, not just to make the existing job marginally faster.
- Roles under ~$100K base. My current scope and AI build output are worth $120K-160K at AI-forward startups and $110K-140K at enterprise AI transformation roles.

## Tone I want to project

Confident, direct, dry. Not "passionate," not "excited," not "energetic." Someone who has walked into messes and made them work, and who's choosing this company because the work matches what I'm best at. The reader should finish the letter thinking "this person would be useful from day one" — not "this person really wants the job."
